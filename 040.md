# Ghafir
## The Forgiver

In the Name of God, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful.

1. Ha, Mim.
2. The sending down of the Book is from God, the Mighty, the Knowing,
3. Forgiver of sin and Accepter of repentance, severe in chastisement, the Possessor of bounty. There is no god but He; unto Him is the journeying.
4. None dispute concerning the signs of God save those who cover over. So let not their going about in the lands delude thee.
5. Before them denied the people of Noah and the parties after them, and every nation purposed against their messenger to seize him, and disputed with falsehood to refute thereby the truth. So I seized them; and how was My chastisement!
6. And thus was the word of thy Lord justified against those who covered over, that they are the companions of the Fire.
7. Those who bear the Throne and those round about it glorify the praise of their Lord and believe in Him and ask forgiveness for those who believe: "Our Lord! Thou encompassest all things in mercy and knowledge; so forgive those who repent and follow Thy way, and shield them from the chastisement of the Blaze.
8. Our Lord! And admit them into the Gardens of Eternity which Thou hast promised them, and those who are righteous of their fathers and their spouses and their offspring. Verily, Thou art the Mighty, the Wise.
9. And shield them from evil deeds. And he whom Thou shieldest from evil deeds on that Day, him hast Thou indeed shown mercy. And that is the supreme triumph."
10. Verily, those who cover over shall be called: "The loathing of God is greater than your loathing of your own selves, when ye were called unto the faith but ye covered over."
11. They say: "Our Lord! Thou hast caused us to die twice, and Thou hast given us life twice; now we confess our sins. Is there then any way of going forth?"
12. "That is because, when God alone was called upon, ye covered over. But when associates were joined with Him, ye believed. So the judgement belongeth to God, the Most High, the Most Great."
13. He it is Who showeth you His signs and sendeth down for you provision from the heaven. And none remembereth save him who turneth in repentance.
14. So call upon God, making your religion sincere unto Him, though those who cover over be averse.
15. Exalter of degrees, Lord of the Throne! He casteth the Spirit of His command upon whom He willeth of His servants, that he may warn of the Day of Meeting,
16. the Day when they come forth; naught of them is hidden from God. "Whose is the dominion this Day?" "God's, the One, the Irresistible!"
17. This Day every soul is recompensed for what it earned. No wrong this Day! Verily, God is swift in reckoning.
18. And warn them of the Day of the Imminent, when the hearts shall be at the throats, choking. The wrongdoers shall have no intimate friend and no intercessor who might be heeded.
19. He knoweth the treachery of the eyes and what the breasts conceal.
20. And God judgeth with truth; and those whom they call upon besides Him judge with naught. Verily, God, He is the Hearing, the Seeing.
21. Have they not journeyed in the earth and seen how was the end of those who were before them? They were mightier than them in strength and in traces upon the earth. But God seized them for their sins, and they had against God no protector.
22. That was because their messengers used to come unto them with clear proofs, but they covered over. So God seized them; verily, He is Strong, severe in chastisement.
23. And verily, We sent Moses with Our signs and a clear authority
24. unto Pharaoh and Haman and Korah; but they said: "A sorcerer, a liar!"
25. Then when he brought them the truth from Us, they said: "Slay the sons of those who believe with him, and spare their women." But the plotting of those who cover over is naught but in error.
26. And Pharaoh said: "Leave me to slay Moses, and let him call upon his Lord. Verily, I fear that he will change your religion or cause corruption to appear in the earth."
27. And Moses said: "Verily, I seek refuge with my Lord and your Lord from every proud one who believeth not in the Day of Reckoning."
28. And a believing man of the house of Pharaoh who concealed his faith said: "Would ye slay a man because he saith: 'My Lord is God,' and he hath come unto you with clear proofs from your Lord? And if he be a liar, upon him is his lie; but if he be truthful, some of that which he threateneth you will befall you. Verily, God guideth not him who is a prodigal, a liar.
29. O my people! Yours is the dominion today, uppermost in the earth; but who will succour us against the might of God, if it cometh upon us?" Pharaoh said: "I show you only what I see, and I guide you only unto the way of right conduct."
30. And he who believed said: "O my people! Verily, I fear for you the like of the day of the parties,
31. the like of the case of the people of Noah and Ad and Thamud and those who came after them. And God willeth no wrong for His servants.
32. And O my people! Verily, I fear for you the Day of Calling Out,
33. the Day when ye shall turn your backs and flee, having no protector from God. And whomsoever God sendeth astray, for him there is no guide.
34. And verily, Joseph came unto you aforetime with clear proofs, but ye ceased not to be in doubt about what he brought you. Until when he perished, ye said: 'God will never send a messenger after him.' Thus God sendeth astray him who is a prodigal, a doubter."
35. Those who dispute concerning the signs of God without any authority that hath come unto them, greatly hateful is it in the sight of God and in the sight of those who believe. Thus God sealeth every proud, tyrannous heart.
36. And Pharaoh said: "O Haman! Build me a tower that I may reach the ropes,
37. the ropes of the heavens, and may look upon the God of Moses; and verily, I deem him a liar." And thus the evil of his deed was made fair-seeming unto Pharaoh, and he was turned from the way. And the plot of Pharaoh was naught but in ruin.
38. And he who believed said: "O my people! Follow me; I will guide you unto the way of right conduct.
39. O my people! This life of the world is but a passing enjoyment, and verily, the Hereafter, it is the abode of permanence.
40. Whosoever doeth an evil deed, he shall be recompensed only the like thereof; and whosoever worketh righteousness, whether male or female, and is a believer, those shall enter the Garden, provided therein without reckoning.
41. And O my people! How is it that I call you unto deliverance, and ye call me unto the Fire?
42. Ye call me to cover over God and to associate with Him that whereof I have no knowledge, while I call you unto the Mighty, the Forgiving.
43. Assuredly, that unto which ye call me hath no claim in this world or in the Hereafter; and our return is unto God; and the prodigal, they are the companions of the Fire.
44. Ye will remember what I say unto you. And I entrust my affair unto God; verily, God is Seeing of His servants."
45. So God shielded him from the evils that they plotted; and there encompassed the house of Pharaoh an evil chastisement:
46. the Fire, unto which they are exposed morning and evening. And on the Day when the Hour cometh: "Admit the house of Pharaoh into the severest chastisement."
47. And when they dispute in the Fire, and the weak say unto those who were proud: "Verily, we were your followers; can ye then avail us of a portion of the Fire?"
48. Those who were proud say: "Verily, we are all therein. Verily, God hath judged between the servants."
49. And those in the Fire say unto the keepers of Hell: "Call upon your Lord that He may lighten for us a day of the chastisement."
50. They say: "Did there not come unto you your messengers with clear proofs?" They say: "Yea." They say: "Then call!" But the calling of those who cover over is naught but in error.
51. Verily, We shall succour Our messengers and those who believe in the life of this world and on the Day when the witnesses arise,
52. the Day when their excuse shall not profit the wrongdoers; and for them is the curse, and for them is the evil abode.
53. And verily, We gave Moses the guidance, and We bequeathed unto the Children of Israel the Book,
54. a guidance and a reminder for those of understanding.
55. So be patient; verily, the promise of God is true. And ask forgiveness for thy sin, and glorify the praise of thy Lord in the evening and in the morning.
56. Verily, those who dispute concerning the signs of God without any authority that hath come unto them, there is naught in their breasts save pride which they shall never attain. So seek refuge with God; verily, He is the Hearing, the Seeing.
57. Verily, the creation of the heavens and the earth is greater than the creation of people; but most of people know not.
58. And not equal are the blind and the seeing, nor those who believe and work righteous deeds and the evildoer. Little do ye remember.
59. Verily, the Hour is coming; there is no doubt thereof. But most of people believe not.
60. And your Lord saith: "Call upon Me and I shall respond unto you. Verily, those who are too proud to worship Me shall enter Hell, humiliated."
61. God it is Who made for you the night that ye may rest therein, and the day sight-giving. Verily, God is full of bounty for people, but most of people give not thanks.
62. That is God, your Lord, Creator of all things. There is no god but He. How then are ye turned away?
63. Thus are turned away those who used to deny the signs of God.
64. God it is Who made for you the earth a resting-place and the heaven a canopy, and fashioned you and made goodly your forms, and provided you with good things. That is God, your Lord. So blessed is God, Lord of the worlds.
65. He is the Living; there is no god but He. So call upon Him, making your religion sincere unto Him. Praise be to God, Lord of the worlds.
66. Say: "Verily, I have been forbidden to worship those whom ye call upon besides God, when the clear proofs have come unto me from my Lord; and I am commanded to surrender unto the Lord of the worlds."
67. He it is Who created you from dust, then from a drop of seed, then from a clot, then bringeth you forth as children, then that ye may reach your full strength, then that ye may become old — though some of you die before — and that ye may reach an appointed term, and that haply ye may understand.
68. He it is Who giveth life and causeth death. Then when He decreeth a thing, He but saith unto it: "Be!" and it is.
69. Hast thou not seen those who dispute concerning the signs of God? How are they turned away?
70. Those who deny the Book and that wherewith We have sent Our messengers — they shall soon know,
71. when the shackles are on their necks and the chains; they are dragged
72. into boiling water, then into the Fire they are kindled.
73. Then it is said unto them: "Where is that which ye used to associate
74. besides God?" They say: "They have strayed from us; nay, we called not upon anything before." Thus God sendeth astray those who cover over.
75. "That is because ye used to exult upon the earth without right, and because ye used to be insolent.
76. Enter the gates of Hell, to abide therein." And wretched is the abode of the proud.
77. So be patient; verily, the promise of God is true. And whether We show thee a part of what We promise them, or We take thee, unto Us they shall be returned.
78. And verily, We sent messengers before thee; among them are those of whom We have told thee, and among them are those of whom We have not told thee. And it was not for any messenger to bring a sign save by the leave of God. Then when the command of God cometh, it is decided in truth; and those who followed falsehood are then the losers.
79. God it is Who hath made for you the cattle, that ye may ride some of them and of some ye eat.
80. And in them for you are benefits; and that ye may reach upon them a need that is in your breasts; and upon them and upon the ships ye are borne.
81. And He showeth you His signs. Then which of the signs of God do ye deny?
82. Have they not journeyed in the earth and seen how was the end of those before them? They were more numerous than these and mightier in strength and in traces upon the earth. But what they used to earn availed them not.
83. Then when their messengers came unto them with clear proofs, they exulted in the knowledge they had, and there encompassed them that which they used to mock.
84. Then when they saw Our might, they said: "We believe in God alone, and we cover over what we used to associate."
85. But their faith, when they saw Our might, profited them not. It is the way of God that hath already passed concerning His servants. And the losers there are those who cover over.

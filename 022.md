# Al-Hajj
## The Pilgrimage

In the Name of God, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful.

1. O people! Fear your Lord. Verily, the earthquake of the Hour is a tremendous thing.
2. On the Day when ye see it, every suckling mother shall forget her suckling, and every pregnant one shall bring forth her burden, and thou shalt see people drunken, though they are not drunken; but the chastisement of God is severe.
3. And among people is he who disputeth concerning God without knowledge and followeth every rebellious devil,
4. against whom it is decreed that whosoever befriendeth him, he shall lead him astray and guide him unto the chastisement of the Blaze.
5. O people! If ye are in doubt about the resurrection, then verily We created you from dust, then from a drop of seed, then from a clot, then from a lump of flesh, formed and unformed, that We might make clear unto you. And We cause what We will to remain in the wombs unto an appointed term; then We bring you forth as infants; then that ye may reach your full strength. And among you is he who is taken early in death, and among you is he who is returned unto the most abject age, so that he knoweth naught after having known. And thou seest the earth barren; then when We send down water upon it, it stirreth and groweth and putteth forth of every beauteous pair.
6. That is because God, He is the Truth, and He giveth life unto the dead, and He is over all things Powerful,
7. and the Hour is coming, there is no doubt thereof, and God shall raise those who are in the graves.
8. And among people is he who disputeth concerning God without knowledge or guidance or an illuminating Book,
9. turning his side to lead astray from the way of God. For him in this world is disgrace, and We shall cause him to taste on the Day of Resurrection the chastisement of the Burning.
10. "That is for what thy hands have sent before, and God is not unjust unto the servants."
11. And among people is he who worshippeth God on an edge. Then if good befalleth him, he is content therewith; and if a trial befalleth him, he turneth on his face. He hath lost this world and the Hereafter. That is the manifest loss.
12. He calleth, besides God, upon that which harmeth him not nor profiteth him. That is the far error.
13. He calleth upon him whose harm is nearer than his benefit. Evil indeed is the patron, and evil indeed is the companion!
14. Verily, God shall admit those who believe and work righteous deeds into Gardens beneath which rivers flow. Verily, God doeth what He willeth.
15. Whosoever thinketh that God will not succour him in this world and the Hereafter, let him stretch a rope unto the heaven, then let him cut it off, and see whether his stratagem removeth that which enrageth him.
16. And thus We have sent it down as clear signs; and God guideth whom He willeth.
17. Verily, those who believe, and those who are of Jewry, and the Sabaeans, and the Christians, and the Magians, and those who associate — verily, God shall decide between them on the Day of Resurrection. Verily, God is over all things a Witness.
18. Hast thou not seen that unto God prostrateth whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is in the earth, and the sun and the moon and the stars and the mountains and the trees and the beasts, and many of people? And many are those upon whom the chastisement is justified. And whomsoever God abaseth, there is none to honour him. Verily, God doeth what He willeth.
19. These are two adversaries who dispute concerning their Lord. Those who cover over, for them garments of fire are cut; boiling water is poured over their heads,
20. whereby that which is in their bellies, and their skins, is melted.
21. And for them are hooked rods of iron.
22. Whenever they desire to come forth therefrom in their anguish, they are driven back therein: "Taste the chastisement of the Burning!"
23. Verily, God shall admit those who believe and work righteous deeds into Gardens beneath which rivers flow. They are adorned therein with bracelets of gold and with pearls; and their garments therein are silk.
24. And they are guided unto goodly speech, and they are guided unto the path of the Praiseworthy.
25. Verily, those who cover over and turn away from the way of God and the Sacred Mosque, which We have made for people, alike the dweller therein and the visitor — and whosoever seeketh therein wrongful deviation, We shall cause him to taste a painful chastisement.
26. And when We settled for Abraham the place of the House: "Associate naught with Me, and purify My House for those who go round it and those who stand and those who bow and prostrate.
27. And proclaim among people the pilgrimage; they shall come unto thee on foot and on every lean camel, coming from every deep ravine,
28. that they may witness benefits for them, and mention the name of God on appointed days over the cattle He hath provided them. So eat thereof and feed the wretched poor.
29. Then let them end their unkemptness and fulfil their vows and go round the Ancient House."
30. That! And whosoever honoureth the sacred things of God, it is better for him with his Lord. And permitted unto you are the cattle, save what is recited unto you. So shun the abomination of idols and shun false speech,
31. being men of pure faith unto God, not associating aught with Him. And whosoever associateth with God, it is as though he had fallen from the heaven and the birds had snatched him, or the wind had blown him to a far-off place.
32. That! And whosoever honoureth the rites of God, verily it is from the piety of hearts.
33. Therein for you are benefits for an appointed term; then their place of sacrifice is at the Ancient House.
34. And for every nation We have appointed a rite, that they may mention the name of God over the cattle He hath provided them. And your God is One God; so unto Him Surrender. And give glad tidings unto the humble,
35. whose hearts tremble when God is mentioned, and those who bear patiently what befalleth them, and those who establish the prayer, and who spend of what We have provided them.
36. And the camels, We have made them for you among the rites of God; therein for you is good. So mention the name of God over them as they stand in ranks; then when they fall down on their sides, eat thereof and feed the content and the suppliant. Thus have We subjected them unto you, that haply ye may give thanks.
37. Their flesh reacheth not God, nor doth their blood, but the piety from you reacheth Him. Thus hath He subjected them unto you, that ye may magnify God for having guided you. And give glad tidings unto the doers of good.
38. Verily, God defendeth those who believe. Verily, God loveth not any treacherous, ungrateful one.
39. Leave is given unto those who are fought because they have been wronged; and verily, God is able to succour them,
40. those who have been driven from their homes unjustly, only because they say: "Our Lord is God." And were it not that God repelleth some people by means of others, then cloisters and churches and synagogues and mosques, wherein the name of God is much mentioned, would have been pulled down. And verily, God helpeth him who helpeth Him. Verily, God is Strong, Mighty,
41. those who, if We establish them in the earth, establish the prayer and pay the alms and enjoin the right and forbid the wrong. And unto God belongeth the end of all affairs.
42. And if they deny thee, then before them denied the people of Noah and Ad and Thamud,
43. and the people of Abraham and the people of Lot,
44. and the companions of Midian. And Moses was denied. Then I granted respite to those who covered over; then I seized them. And how was My denial!
45. And how many a township We destroyed while it was doing wrong, and it is now fallen upon its roofs; and how many a well is deserted, and how many a lofty castle!
46. Have they not journeyed in the earth, so that they may have hearts wherewith to understand, or ears wherewith to hear? Verily, it is not the eyes that are blind, but blind are the hearts that are in the breasts.
47. And they bid thee hasten the chastisement; and God will never fail His promise. And verily, a day with thy Lord is as a thousand years of what ye reckon.
48. And how many a township I granted respite while it was doing wrong. Then I seized it; and unto Me is the journeying.
49. Say: "O people! I am but a clear warner unto you."
50. Then those who believe and work righteous deeds, for them is forgiveness and a generous provision.
51. And those who strive against Our signs to frustrate them, those are the companions of the Blaze.
52. And We sent not before thee any messenger or prophet but when he desired, the devil cast into his desire. Then God abolisheth what the devil casteth; then God establisheth His signs. And God is Knowing, Wise,
53. that He may make what the devil casteth a trial for those in whose hearts is a sickness and those whose hearts are hardened — and verily the wrongdoers are in far schism —
54. and that those who have been given knowledge may know that it is the truth from thy Lord and believe therein and their hearts may be humble unto it. And verily, God guideth those who believe unto a straight path.
55. And those who cover over shall not cease to be in doubt thereof until the Hour cometh upon them suddenly, or there cometh upon them the chastisement of a barren Day.
56. The dominion on that Day is God's; He shall judge between them. Then those who believe and work righteous deeds are in the Gardens of Delight.
57. And those who covered over and denied Our signs, those, for them is a humiliating chastisement.
58. And those who emigrate in the way of God, then are slain or die, God will surely provide them a fair provision; and verily, God, He is the best of providers.
59. He shall surely admit them into a place that pleaseth them; and verily, God is Knowing, Forbearing.
60. That! And whosoever retalitateth with the like of what he was made to suffer, then is wronged again, God will surely succour him. Verily, God is Pardoning, Forgiving.
61. That is because God causeth the night to enter into the day and causeth the day to enter into the night, and that God is Hearing, Seeing.
62. That is because God, He is the Truth, and that which they call upon besides Him, it is the false. And that God, He is the Most High, the Most Great.
63. Hast thou not seen that God sendeth down water from the heaven, and the earth becometh green? Verily, God is Subtle, Aware.
64. Unto Him belongeth whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is in the earth; and verily, God, He is the Self-Sufficient, the Praiseworthy.
65. Hast thou not seen that God hath subjected unto you whatsoever is in the earth, and the ships that run upon the sea by His command? And He holdeth back the heaven from falling upon the earth save by His leave. Verily, God is unto people Gentle, Merciful.
66. And He it is Who gave you life, then causeth you to die, then giveth you life. Verily, man is ungrateful.
67. For every nation We have appointed a rite which they observe. So let them not dispute with thee about the matter; and call unto thy Lord. Verily, thou art upon straight guidance.
68. And if they dispute with thee, say: "God is most knowing of what ye do.
69. God shall judge between you on the Day of Resurrection concerning that wherein ye used to differ."
70. Hast thou not known that God knoweth whatsoever is in the heaven and the earth? Verily, that is in a Book. Verily, that is easy for God.
71. And they worship, besides God, that for which He hath sent down no authority, and that whereof they have no knowledge. And for the wrongdoers there is no helper.
72. And when Our signs are recited unto them as clear proofs, thou knowest in the faces of those who cover over the denial. They wellnigh assail those who recite unto them Our signs. Say: "Shall I inform you of what is worse than that? The Fire! God hath promised it unto those who cover over. And wretched is the journeying."
73. O people! A likeness is coined, so hearken unto it. Verily, those whom ye call upon besides God will never create a fly, even though they gathered together for it. And if the fly should snatch away something from them, they could not recover it therefrom. Weak is the seeker and the sought!
74. They have not measured God with His true measure. Verily, God is Strong, Mighty.
75. God chooseth from the angels messengers, and from people. Verily, God is Hearing, Seeing.
76. He knoweth what is before them and what is behind them; and unto God all affairs are returned.
77. O ye who believe! Bow and prostrate and worship your Lord and do good, that haply ye may prosper.
78. And strive for God as is His due. He hath chosen you and hath laid upon you no hardship in the religion, the creed of your father Abraham. He named you the Surrendered, before and in this, that the Messenger may be a witness over you, and that ye may be witnesses over people. So establish the prayer and pay the alms and hold fast unto God. He is your Protector. How excellent a Protector, and how excellent a Helper!

# Scholar translation of the Qur'an

1. Annotate the existing translations in separate files number-comments.md, e.g. 55-comments.md
2. Try to stick as close as possible to the structure of the original, never ignoring particles that often are removed in modern translations. These can be meaningful for its deeper meaning.
3. When all translations agree, stick to this translation, only making the structure fit better to the original grammar.
4. Stick to a Shoghi Effendi English / KJV English kind of style.
5. Whenever a word is rare or might be in common use only because of the Qur'an, it likely suffers from a common interpretation because of trying to interpret the Qur'an.
   Whenever this happens, check with similar words with the same original Arabic root and try to figure out a more likely word out of this root. If the meaning in context improves, you have a likely candidate.
6. Look out for puns, like the sound proximity of Alaa with Alí in Ar-Rahmán, and put these finds in the comments.
7. Prefer an integrative reading: link Qur'anic imagery to the present day (e.g. the unharmed barrier between the seas as global communication), and understand surahs and verses in the context of each other as a unified whole.
